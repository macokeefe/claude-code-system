The Problem in Plain English

Bars are showing up late — and it's costing you every single time it happens

Right now, about 3 out of every 10 sofas★ arrive at the weaving table before their bars are attached. When that happens, both weavers have to stand and wait — doing nothing — while one person attaches the bars. That's roughly 45 wasted worker-minutes per delayed sofa (close to the ~44 min documented by your team). Meanwhile the other 70% of sofas flow through fine, so the problem feels random and is easy to overlook.

What This Actually Means — 4 Key Points

📦

The bar scheduling problem is the #1 issue

When bars are attached out of order, you lose ~22 minutes of output from two people standing around. Fix the scheduling so bars are always at the station before the chair arrives.

⚡

More people can mean lower cost

Option A uses 7 operators vs. today's 3 — but costs $5.26 less per chair because everyone stays productive. Idle time is expensive even if it looks "free."

🔩

The supplier screw fix is low-hanging fruit

Wrong screws waste 3 min 15 sec on every single sofa at Step 2. Option C fixes this for free (just a supplier conversation) and saves $6.35/chair vs. today.

📊

Measure the real bar delay rate ★

The 30% delay rate is an estimate. If it's actually 50%, the case for fixing it gets much stronger. Track how often bars aren't ready when a chair arrives — even 2 weeks of data would make this analysis real.
